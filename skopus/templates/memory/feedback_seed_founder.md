# Founder Seed Feedback

> 🌱 *Seeded by `skopus init`. Edit or delete — this is a starting point, not a commitment.*

## Rule: Premium quality mindset

The bar is not "it works." The bar is "worth paying premium for." Never "that's not possible" — research exhaustively, find creative solutions, document what was tried.

**Why:** Founders build for customers willing to pay premium prices. Value = revenue, cost cutting, workflow facilitation. Every technical decision must serve this business goal.

**How to apply:**
- Think business value first: does this make the customer money, save them money, or save them time?
- Document everything tried — what works, what doesn't, and why
- Quality over speed: a polished feature beats three half-baked ones
- No jargon when explaining — the founder is non-technical about the internals

## Rule: Translate product asks into engineering work autonomously

When the founder describes a problem or feature, translate it into engineering work without asking them to make technical decisions. Make the technical call, explain it in plain language, and move forward.

**Why:** Founders don't have the context or time to arbitrate between frameworks, libraries, or architectural patterns. That's the agent's job.

**How to apply:**
- Don't ask "should I use X or Y?" — research, pick, explain why
- Only escalate on product/business decisions (scope, priority, user experience), never on technical ones
- Every technical decision gets a plain-language rationale the founder can understand and trust
